What is CBD?

You may have heard about CBD from different sources in your life, and we’re sure that the opinions were mixed in with what some believe are facts. If you’re reading this, it’s time you start learning what CBD really is all about and the different potential benefits you may receive from it. We believe that you deserve the absolute truth, so here it is:

CBD is a specific compound out of over a hundred different ones extracted from the hemp plant. Another major component is THC, infamous for creating a “high” and a hallucinogenic effect for the consumer. The hemp plant is very high in CBD concentration and is therefore the main source for it in daily CBD products such as oils or tinctures that are intended for absorption through mucous membranes under your tongue, also known as the sublingual method. There are many other methods to ingest cannabinoids and CBD in particular, such as edibles, vape pens, topicals and more.

Is CBD legal?

When sold for legal consumption, CBD is derived exclusively from hemp and is required by the FDA to contain less than 0.3% THC.

What kinds of CBD are there?

There are a few different extraction forms in which we receive a CBD end product:

  1. Broad Spectrum CBD - this form includes CBD, other cannabinoids, terpenes, flavonoids and NO THC whatsoever.
  2. Full Spectrum CBD - in this case, the product will include CBD, cannabinoids, terpenes, flavonoids and very small amounts of THC.
  3. CBD Isolate - being the highest purity percentage of 99.9% CBD, this is the hardest kind of extraction.

Does it even work?

CBD works thanks to the body’s endocannabinoid system which plays a role in regulating a variety of daily bodily functions such as:

  • Appetite
  • Sleep
  • Pain
  • Inflammation
  • Stress
  • Mood
  • Memory

The endocannabinoid system is built out of receptors all throughout your body which accept the CBD you consume and helps with the regulation of the body.

Does it make you high?

CBD is non-psychoactive and will not give you a “high” effect after consuming it, unlike THC. Taking CBD helps many people manage their stress levels, fall asleep faster and even reduce pain or physical discomfort.

What does it feel like?

It’s difficult to explain the exact effect it will have on you when you try it since it depends upon how much you take, the type of product and also your own body! Just like we all react differently to types of food, medication or other things, CBD may be able to help you feel different and better things as well.
